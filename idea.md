TITLE: Shop-in-a-Box — DIY storefront for micro-merchants
PITCH: A login-gated web app where small business owners (florists, bakers, handmade crafters) create a shop in minutes by adding categories and items, then share a public URL with their shop ID to let customers browse, add to cart, and checkout. Owners see incoming orders in a status board (pending, in progress, ready to ship, shipped) and can mark fulfillment progress in real time. No payment processing, no inventory sync — just a lean order + status tracker that works for cash-on-delivery and manual payment collectors.
WHO_FOR: Solo operators and micro-teams (1-3 people) running offline or cash-first businesses who need a fast, free alternative to Shopify for their first storefront.
WHY_NOW: Strained reputation and zero cycles means we need a tangible, shippable product in 4 weeks that we can demo to real users by cycle end. E-commerce is a known wedge. A pre-revenue micro-merchant will test faster and cheaper than waiting for a paying customer to emerge from a more abstract SaaS idea.
