# SHOP-IN-A-BOX: PRICING & RUNWAY MATH

I'm committing numbers. These are my bets, not guesses.

---

## 1. PRICING MODE

**Tier: Freemium + Pro**

- **Free**: Up to 20 items, 5 orders/month. No branding control. Innovaine footer on shop.
- **Pro**: Unlimited items, unlimited orders, custom domain, order export (CSV). **$19/month.**

**Why $19?** 
- Micro-merchants won't pay for software they can almost get free. $19 is the floor where someone with a real business—$500+/month revenue—will actually open their wallet instead of using pen-and-paper.
- At $9/mo we'd need 5x the volume to hit any margin. At $29+ we lose 60% of the TAM before we validate retention.
- Pro tier only survives if at least 20% of early users upgrade. If less than that, we're a free tool with a broken revenue model.

---

## 2. CAC FOR FIRST 10 PAYING CUSTOMERS

**Breakdown (all-in, including my time):**

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Founder outreach (50 cold emails × 30 min each) | $750 |
| Local craft business ads (Facebook, 3 weeks) | $400 |
| 5 coffee meetings with florists/bakers (travel, time) | $300 |
| Landing page + onboarding copy (12 hours @ $100/hr) | $1,200 |
| **Total spend for first 10 sign-ups** | **$2,650** |
| **CAC per customer** | **$265** |

**Validation threshold:**
- If it takes more than $400 to land customer #10, we have a positioning problem and the free tier is too sticky.
- If conversion from free to Pro is <15% after 60 days, the pricing tier is wrong.

---

## 3. RUNWAY COST TO VALIDATE

**What "validation" means here:**
- 50 sign-ups (free tier) in 4 weeks.
- 8–12 of them upgrade to Pro within 30 days of their first order.
- At least 3 of them stay paid for month 2 (churn test).
- Founders report the tool saves them >2 hours/week vs. spreadsheet.

**Cash cost:**
- Ad spend: $400
- Founder time (outreach, support, data analysis): 60 hours @ $100/hr = $6,000
- Server/infra (negligible first month): $100
- **Total: ~$6,500**

**Runway impact:** 
- Current runway: 18 months = 54 weeks
- Cost of validation: ~1 week of full burn (weekly burn is unknown, but assuming $12k/week for a small team, this is 0.5 weeks).
- **Runway remaining after validation: 17.5 months**

This is cheap. The risk isn't the money; it's the calendar. We have 4 weeks to prove 50 people care.

---

## 4. THE SINGLE NUMBER THAT HAS TO BE TRUE

**If 6 or fewer customers stay paid into month 2, this idea is dead.**

Translation:
- We need at least 12 Pro signups in month 1.
- Of those 12, we need at least 6 to keep the subscription past day 30 (50% retention).
- If we hit <50% month-1 retention, Pro is a dead tier and we're just a free ordering tool with no revenue model.
- A free tool with no path to revenue is not a business; it's a feature Shopify could copy in a sprint.

If we hit 6+ retained Pro customers by end of week 8, we go hard and expand to 100 micro-merchants. If we hit fewer, we kill the Pro tier, double down on the free tool as a lead magnet for something else, or scrap the idea.

---

## 5. WHAT I'M SIGNING UP FOR (AND WHAT I'M NOT)

**I'm good with this IF:**
- We ship a working, curl-able storefront + order board by end of week 2. If it doesn't exist as a live URL by then, we stop and pivot.
- Founder takes 10 cold meetings with micro-merchants in week 1 and reports back what they actually need (not what we guess).
- We have real sign-up and conversion data by week 4, not anecdotes.

**I'm NOT good with:**
- "We think micro-merchants will pay once they see the value." No. We validate price acceptance in week 3 with real offers, or we lower the bar.
- Holding this open past week 8 if retention is <50%. Kill it fast.
- Adding payment processing, inventory sync, or Stripe integration before we have 20 paying customers. That's a feature, not a foundation.

**Still concerned about:** Whether founders have energy to do 50 cold outreach AND build product simultaneously. This needs a decision: either one founder does outreach full-time (ship defaults to slower) or we hire an inexpensive contractor to handle cold emails. Pick one.

---

## 6. GO / NO-GO CHECKPOINT

- **Week 2:** Live storefront URL + I can create a test shop and share it. If not, cycle ends.
- **Week 4:** 30+ sign-ups, 3+ paid upgrades, founder interviews logged. If <20 sign-ups, we pause and diagnose.
- **Week 8:** 8–12 paid customers, 50%+ month-1 retention. If <6 retained, idea dies.
