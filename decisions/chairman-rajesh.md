APPROVED WITH PIVOT: Shop-in-a-Box, but v1 is one merchant, one product category, one checkout flow — no multi-item cart, no order status board, no user auth. A florist uploads 5 flowers, you get a public link, customers click "buy," you get an email. Ship that in 7 days.

**Reasoning:**

Your pitch has the right shape — fast, shippable, real users by week 4, zero payment processing burden. But you've packed too much into the minimum. The status board, multi-category UX, order management dashboard — that's cycle-2 work. Week 1 goal is "can a real merchant share a link with a customer and receive an order notification." That's it. If that works, you've validated the wedge and have paying users or partnership interest by week 3. If it doesn't, you've learned it in 7 days, not 28.

This also solves the reputation problem directly: you'll have a live, clickable storefront and a real merchant testimonial faster than anything more architecturally ambitious. One cycle with momentum beats four weeks of debate about order workflows.

Go live with a static link generator + email notification. No auth, no dashboard, no inventory. When a customer clicks "buy," they see a form, you get the submission in your inbox. Florist manually replies with payment instructions. Boring? Yes. Shippable? Yes. Real? Yes.
